# Software Engineer, Autocap

**Company:** Google
**Location:** Sunnyvale, CA
**Seniority:** Entry, Mid Level
**Work Model:** Onsite
**Employment Type:** Full-time

## About the job
Google is seeking a Software Engineer for the Autocap team, which focuses on automated capacity management across all Google production services. The role involves designing and developing backend services, collaborating with various teams, and improving system performance and reliability.

## Core Skills
- Software Development
- Back-end Software Development
- Distributed Systems
- Infrastructure Development
- Cluster Management Systems

## Minimum qualifications
- Bachelor's degree or equivalent practical experience
- 2 years of experience in general software development
- 2 years of experience with back-end software development
- 1 year of experience with distributed systems

## Preferred qualifications
- Ph.D. in Computer Science
- Operations Research
- Mathematics, or a related technical field
- 3 years of experience in infrastructure development
- Experience with large-scale production environments and technologies, such as cluster management systems
- Prodspec
- Annealing
- Experience with data analysis, time-series data, or optimization algorithms
- Experience building scalable, reliable, and efficient distributed systems, and the knowledge of systems design
- Familiarity with capacity management concepts, resource forecasting, or service placement challenges

## Responsibilities
- Design, develop, test, deploy, and maintain backend services and components within the Autocap capacity management ecosystem
- Contribute to the architecture and implementation of scalable and reliable infrastructure for demand forecasting, resource modeling, supply management, and footprint optimization
- Collaborate with team members and partner teams (e.g.
- Spatial Flex
- Rollouts
- Server Platform
- Flex) to integrate Autocap with other production systems and enhance end-to-end automation
- Improve system performance, reliability, and efficiency by refactoring code, optimizing algorithms, and addressing technical debt
- Contribute to the team's technical roadmap and help shape the future of automated capacity management at Google
- Write and review design documents and high-quality code, adhering to Google's engineering best practices and standards
