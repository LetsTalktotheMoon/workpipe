# Software Engineer II - BIC Foundations

**Company:** Microsoft
**Location:** Redmond, WA
**Seniority:** Entry, Mid Level
**Work Model:** Onsite
**Employment Type:** Full-time

## About the job
Microsoft is a leader in Software as a Service, and they are seeking a Software Engineer II for the Business & Industry Copilot Foundations team. This role involves designing and developing scalable cloud services and APIs, integrating AI technologies, and ensuring service reliability and performance.

## Core Skills
- C
- C++
- C#
- Java
- JavaScript

## Minimum qualifications
- Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science or related technical field AND 2+ years technical engineering experience with coding in languages including, but not limited to
- C
- C++
- C#
- Java
- JavaScript, or Python OR equivalent experience
- Ability to meet Microsoft, customer and/or government security screening requirements are required for this role. These requirements include but are not limited to the following specialized security screenings: Microsoft Cloud Background Check: This position will be required to pass the Microsoft Cloud background check upon hire/transfer and every two years thereafter

## Preferred qualifications
- 1+ years of experience with generative AI OR LLM/ML algorithms
- 3+ years of experience developing and deploying live production systems
- Experience with Microsoft stack technologies (Azure / .NET)
- Familiarity with modern LLMOps frameworks (e.g.
- LangChain
- PromptFlow)
- Experience across the product lifecycle from ideation to shipping
- Experience with container technologies (Docker / Kubernetes)

## Responsibilities
- Design, develop, test, and maintain high-quality, scalable cloud services and APIs that support Business & Industry Copilot scenarios and business‑critical workloads
- Collaborate with product managers, partner engineering teams, and stakeholders to understand requirements and translate them into well‑designed technical solutions
- Implement features and services using modern software engineering practices, including clean architecture, reusable components, and automated testing
- Contribute to design discussions and technical reviews, producing design documentation and participating in code reviews to uphold engineering quality standards
- Own features and components end‑to‑end, from development through deployment and live‑site support, operating within a DevOps model
- Monitor, troubleshoot, and resolve production issues, participating in on‑call rotations and supporting service reliability, availability, and performance
- Use telemetry, logging, and monitoring to drive data‑informed decisions and continuous service improvement
